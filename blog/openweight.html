What open-weight LLMs today can (and can't) do

The recent wave of open-weight LLMs are leveling the playing field ‚Äì allowing developers to build robust, privacy-friendly and cost-efficient NLP applications with full control and ownership. Instead of relying on a closed model like GPT4, Claude or Gemini, you now have the luxury of choice when picking an open model to run/deploy locally or on your favourite cloud.

But which open model suits your application best?

ü¶ú For multi-linguality, go with the recent DeepSeek AI v3, Google DeepMind's Gemma 3 (except 1b) or Qwen 3, which are all supporting more than 100 languages. If you need to cover multi-modality, there's a huge variety to pick from, including Gemma 3 or DeepSeek Janus Pro.

üìö If you need a looooong context window, Meta's Llama 4 has got you covered with Maverick (1M) & Scout (10M !!!). In fact, most recent open models support at least 128K tokens, which is pretty much a full novel.

üêû Size matters ‚Äì so if you want to run on mobile or edge devices, you can pick any of the 1B models (e.g. Qwen 3, Falcon 3, Llama 3), a SmolLM by Hugging Face, or a larger one if your hardware can handle it. Remember, a quantized version of a larger model often works better than an original smaller model.
